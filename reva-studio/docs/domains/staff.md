# Staff Domain

## Status

Approved

## Context

Reva Studio is a beauty-service platform. The `staff` domain is responsible for modeling professionals who provide services inside a tenant, their employment status, competencies, working capacity, scheduling eligibility, assignment constraints, and business availability for bookings.

This document defines the domain model, ubiquitous language, invariants, aggregate boundaries, commands, events, and integration rules for the `staff` domain.

The design of this document follows Domain-Driven Design principles where a bounded context defines where a model applies, and aggregate roots are the consistency boundary and the only update entry point for the aggregate. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Goals

The `staff` domain must:

- represent a staff member as a business entity, not as an authentication account;
- preserve business invariants around service eligibility, employment state, and booking readiness;
- provide a clean source of truth for who can perform which services and under which constraints;
- support multi-tenant isolation;
- support future growth from a small beauty studio to a partner-based platform;
- integrate cleanly with bookings, services catalog, payroll, notifications, and analytics.

## Non-Goals

The `staff` domain does not own:

- authentication credentials;
- payroll calculations;
- final appointment booking records;
- chat or notification delivery;
- media storage for portfolio assets;
- fine-grained calendar slots generated by the scheduling engine.

Those concerns belong to other bounded contexts and may reference staff only through stable identifiers. Bounded contexts should remain explicit, and aggregate access must go through aggregate roots rather than bypassing them. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Ubiquitous Language

### Core Terms

- Staff Member: a professional or internal worker associated with a tenant.
- Master: a service-performing specialist such as manicure, lashes, brows, massage, makeup.
- Administrator: a non-performing employee with operational permissions.
- Employment Status: the current business relationship state of a staff member.
- Service Capability: permission and qualification of a staff member to perform a specific service.
- Booking Eligibility: whether a staff member is currently allowed to receive bookings.
- Work Profile: the public and operational representation of the staff member.
- Capacity Policy: booking-related operational limits for a staff member.
- Assignment: association between a staff member and an allowed service.
- Suspension: temporary business block for accepting bookings.
- Deactivation: terminal or long-term removal from active business operations.
- Tenant: salon or business unit that owns the staff member record.

### Naming Rules

Inside this domain:

- `staff member` means the business entity;
- `user` means authentication actor and belongs to the identity or accounts domain;
- `service capability` means approved ability to perform a service;
- `availability` means business eligibility, not raw calendar slots.

## Domain Scope

The `staff` domain owns:

- staff lifecycle;
- public and operational staff profile;
- service assignments and eligibility;
- role category inside the salon business model;
- booking readiness flags and operational restrictions;
- staff-specific limits required before scheduling;
- staff-level soft archival and deactivation.

The `staff` domain publishes facts that other domains consume, for example:

- bookings checks whether a staff member is active and eligible;
- services catalog checks whether a service can be assigned;
- notifications uses staff contact preferences if exposed through an anti-corruption layer;
- analytics counts active staff and specialization distribution.

## Domain Principles

1. A staff member is a business aggregate, not a row-shaped data holder.
2. All mutations must go through explicit business methods on the aggregate root.
3. Invariants must be protected inside the aggregate boundary.
4. Cross-domain references must use identifiers, not direct object coupling.
5. Booking systems may not assign services to staff by bypassing this domain.
6. Tenant isolation is mandatory.
7. Public visibility and booking eligibility are separate concepts.

Rich domain behavior and invariant enforcement through aggregate roots are established DDD practices. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Bounded Context Placement

`staff` is a dedicated bounded context.

It integrates with:

- `identity` or `accounts`
- `services_catalog`
- `bookings`
- `notifications`
- `analytics`
- `payroll`
- `tenant_management`

The model in this context is authoritative only for staff business state. Other contexts must not redefine staff lifecycle semantics internally without an explicit mapping layer. A bounded context exists exactly to prevent one model from leaking ambiguously into another. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

## Aggregate Design

## Aggregate Root: StaffMember

`StaffMember` is the aggregate root and the only entry point for updates to the staff aggregate.

It owns consistency for:

- lifecycle state;
- role category;
- service capabilities;
- booking eligibility;
- public visibility state;
- operational restrictions;
- tenant ownership.

This follows the DDD rule that the aggregate root is the consistency guardian and the only update gate for the aggregate. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

### Aggregate Boundary

Inside the `StaffMember` aggregate:

- StaffProfile
- ProfessionalTitle
- ContactSnapshot
- EmploymentState
- BookingPolicy
- VisibilityPolicy
- ServiceCapability assignments
- SuspensionState
- Audit metadata relevant to domain behavior

Outside the aggregate:

- authentication account;
- payroll contract;
- actual booking calendar;
- uploaded media objects;
- external certificates storage;
- reviews and ratings.

## Invariants

The aggregate must always enforce the following invariants.

### Identity and Ownership Invariants

1. A staff member belongs to exactly one tenant.
2. Cross-tenant reassignment is not an in-place mutation. It requires new aggregate creation and explicit migration workflow.
3. Aggregate references to external domains use IDs only.

### Lifecycle Invariants

4. `archived` staff cannot become publicly visible.
5. `terminated` staff cannot be booking-eligible.
6. `draft` staff cannot be booking-eligible.
7. `suspended` staff cannot accept bookings.
8. `inactive` staff cannot accept bookings.
9. `active` is necessary but not sufficient for booking eligibility.

### Capability Invariants

10. A staff member cannot perform a service unless a matching `ServiceCapability` is `active`.
11. Duplicate active capability for the same `service_id` is forbidden.
12. Revoked capability cannot remain effective in booking eligibility checks.
13. Pending capability must not be treated as active.

### Visibility Invariants

14. Public visibility requires:
    - tenant ownership;
    - non-archived state;
    - non-terminated state;
    - explicit visibility flag.
15. Public visibility does not automatically imply booking eligibility.

### Booking Invariants

16. Booking eligibility requires:
    - `employment_status = active`;
    - `suspension_state.is_suspended = false`;
    - at least one active service capability for service-performing roles;
    - booking policy allows bookings.
17. `max_parallel_bookings` must be greater than or equal to 1 when bookings are enabled.
18. Staff with non-performing role categories may remain non-bookable even if active.

DDD guidance states that invariants are enforced inside the aggregate boundary and updates should go through the aggregate root to preserve consistency. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

## Derived Business Rules

These are computed rules, not necessarily persisted fields.

### `is_bookable`

True only when all conditions hold:

- employment status is active;
- not suspended;
- booking policy allows bookings;
- role category supports bookings;
- at least one active service capability exists.

### `is_public_catalog_candidate`

True only when:

- active or temporarily inactive by allowed business rule;
- visibility policy allows listing;
- not archived;
- not terminated.

### `can_accept_service(service_id)`

True only when:

- `is_bookable = true`
- matching active capability exists for `service_id`

## State Machine

### EmploymentStatus transitions

- `draft -> active`
- `active -> inactive`
- `active -> suspended`
- `inactive -> active`
- `suspended -> active`
- `active -> terminated`
- `inactive -> terminated`
- `suspended -> terminated`
- `terminated -> archived`

Forbidden transitions:

- `archived -> active`
- `terminated -> active` by simple toggle
- `draft -> suspended`
- `draft -> archived` without explicit administrative archival policy
- any state change that bypasses required invariants

## Cross-Domain Integration Rules

### With `services_catalog`

- `staff` stores only `service_id` references;
- service existence and lifecycle are validated through application service or policy layer;
- if a service is deleted or retired, a separate integration process must revoke affected capabilities.

### With `bookings`

- bookings must query bookability through a published read model or domain service;
- bookings must not infer bookability from raw employment status alone;
- bookings must not assign a service the staff member is not explicitly approved for.

### With `identity`

- one staff member may be linked to zero or one user accounts depending on business rules;
- account disabling does not automatically terminate staff employment;
- user account model must not overwrite staff business state.

### With `payroll`

- payroll consumes staff identifiers and status facts;
- payroll must not mutate service capabilities or booking policy directly.

## Domain Services

A domain service is allowed only when logic does not naturally belong to one aggregate.

Candidate domain services:

- `StaffBookabilityPolicy`
- `StaffCapabilityPolicy`
- `StaffLifecyclePolicy`

Examples of when a domain service is justified:

- evaluating a policy that depends on external domain facts while preserving aggregate purity;
- validating cross-aggregate rules in an orchestration-safe way.

## Validation Model

### Syntactic Validation

Handled at DTO or application boundary:

- required field presence;
- string length;
- format checks for email or phone;
- enum membership.

### Semantic Validation

Handled in domain:

- invalid lifecycle transition;
- duplicate capability assignment;
- enabling bookings for non-eligible staff;
- visibility activation for archived or terminated staff;
- negative or zero booking capacity.

The domain model should own semantic business invariants rather than pushing them into controllers or handlers. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

## Multi-Tenant Requirements

Mandatory rules:

- every aggregate carries `tenant_id`;
- all repository lookups are tenant-scoped;
- cross-tenant reads and writes are forbidden by default;
- domain events include tenant identity;
- read models must partition by tenant;
- background processes that touch staff must also enforce tenant scope.

## Audit Requirements

The domain should preserve business-relevant audit facts:

- who initiated the change when relevant;
- when the state changed;
- previous and new business state for high-impact transitions;
- reason for suspension, deactivation, termination, or archival.

This does not require the aggregate to become an append-only log, but high-impact transitions must remain traceable.

## Security and Authorization Notes

Authorization is not owned by the aggregate itself, but the domain requires these guardrails from surrounding layers:

- only authorized staff admins may mutate lifecycle and capability assignments;
- self-service profile editing must be explicitly scoped;
- cross-tenant privilege escalation must be impossible;
- public exposure of internal notes is forbidden.

## Example Business Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Master Onboarding

1. Create draft staff member.
2. Fill profile.
3. Assign allowed services.
4. Approve capabilities.
5. Set booking policy.
6. Activate staff member.
7. Enable public visibility if desired.

Expected result:
- staff becomes active;
- staff is bookable only if capability and booking invariants hold.

### Scenario 2: Temporary Suspension

1. Staff member is active and bookable.
2. Administrator suspends due to leave or incident.
3. Aggregate transitions to suspended.
4. Booking eligibility becomes false.

Expected result:
- no new bookings should be assigned through compliant integrations.

### Scenario 3: Service Revocation

1. Active master loses permission to perform a service.
2. Capability is revoked.
3. Bookings context sees updated eligibility for that service.

Expected result:
- staff may remain bookable for other active services only.

## Edge Cases

- Administrator role with active employment but no bookable services.
- Featured public profile that is visible but not currently bookable.
- Staff profile without linked user account.
- Terminated staff retained for history and analytics but excluded from operations.
- Suspended staff with future suspension end date that still requires explicit resume policy.
- Capability pending approval while staff is otherwise active.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- storing raw mutable flags without aggregate methods;
- allowing booking engine to decide service eligibility independently;
- mixing user account and staff aggregate into one model;
- treating public visibility as equivalent to availability;
- bypassing tenant scope in repository methods;
- using direct SQL updates that skip domain invariants;
- pushing semantic business rules entirely into controllers.

## Acceptance Criteria

This domain definition is considered implemented when:

- `StaffMember` exists as an aggregate root;
- all business mutations pass through explicit domain methods;
- booking eligibility is derived from invariant-safe state;
- service capability duplication is prevented;
- archived and terminated staff cannot become bookable;
- multi-tenant scoping is enforced in repository access;
- read models exist for admin list, public card, and booking eligibility;
- integration tests cover lifecycle transitions and capability revocation.
